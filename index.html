Overview

Warrior is a melee-based class with mostly skills instead of spells.

It's main resource is Rage, wich goes from 0 to 100 and it's only generated either when you hit an enemy or get hit. Your melee (white) critical hits have a bonus Rage generation.

Warriors can use every weapon type(wich sometimes can be tedious to level up the skill whenever you get an upgrade), and every type of armor.

Now for roles, this class can be either DPS or Tank, and they are pretty good at either one, since their scaling with gear (mostly weapons) is really good to cover the damage output, they can also exceed at stacking defensive stats since they can wear plate and shield.

We will focus on the DPS aspect of this class on this document. Let's take a look at the different specs first.

Specs

Warriors have 3 different and very strong specs:

  • Arms
  • Fury
  • Protection

All 3 of them exceed on their role, 2 of them being damage oriented (Arms and Fury), the other being focused on tanking (Protection).

As said before we will be focusing on the DPS aspect of the class, more specifically on the Fury tree, since it's the strongest on PvE. Next we'll see wich stats benefit our spec the most.

Stats

Warriors benefit from multiple stats when it comes to damage output, let's see a little of what each one does:

  • Strength:

    • This stat is pretty straight forward, for each point of strength you gain 2 Attack Power. This is very high on priority due to it's direct scaling with the main fury warrior skill Bloodthirst.

  • Agility:

    • Agility is sort of a secondary skill since you need 20 points to gain 1% crit chance. It's specially good since you want to keep the most uptime on Flurry.

  • Stamina:

    • This keeps you alive, since for every point you gain 10 HP. Since you can wear plate armor, there's some good survivability-over-dps options that have a good balance between the two aspects, wich is important since you can't deal any damage if you're dead.

  • Crit Chance:

    • Specially important for the same reason explained above, you want to keep Flurry uptime since melee swings are the primary source of a warrior's damage.

  • Hit Chance:

    • This is kind of an odd stat, since it generates a lot of confusion when it comes to warrior's hit table (subject I will not dive into in this document). Basically you want either 6 or 9 of it depending on your weapon skill. If you have 305 (either you're human with swords or maces or orc with axes or have access to items like Edgemaster's Handguards) you want 6, if not you need 9 for a level 63 (boss/skull) enemy.
